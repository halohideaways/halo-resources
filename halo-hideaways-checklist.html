Most hosts focus only on reviews. The algorithm goes much deeper — it's scoring your conversion rate, your 90-day trend, your response speed, your support ticket count, and your API sync health. Run through this checklist every quarter.

Search visibility 4 items
View-to-booking conversion rate is above 2% — check this in your listing stats dashboard
Listing price is competitive vs the local STR market — not in constant discount mode
90-day performance trend is on an incline, not declining or flat
Listing appears in the correct categories and search filters for your property type
Listing quality 5 items
Cover photo is the most compelling image — the algorithm tracks which photos guests dwell on longest
Photo order holds attention through the full gallery without drop-off
Description is complete with no missing fields, placeholder text, or outdated information
All amenity tags are fully filled in and accurate to what's actually in the property
Key details are easy to find — a guest shouldn't need to scroll far to understand what they're booking
Host performance 5 items
Message response time is consistently under 1 hour — this directly affects your search ranking
Guest questions are answered fully in the first reply — no follow-up needed to get basic info
All public review responses are factual and professional — future guests are the real audience
Support tickets are minimised — every ticket registers as a negative service signal in the algorithm, regardless of context
No unresolved escalations currently open with Airbnb support
Review protection 5 items
All guest communication is kept inside the native Airbnb chat — WhatsApp and SMS screenshots are dismissed by automated agents
Evidence for any damage claim gathered before filing — timestamped photos via Breezeway or Turno
Damage claims timed carefully — filing near the end of the 14-day review window leaves the guest no time to compose a retaliatory response
Any guest threat to leave a bad review in exchange for a refund is documented inside the native chat before you respond
A structured appeal template is ready to go — formal, factual, and referencing the Retaliatory Reviews & Bias policy
PMS & API sync 4 items
After every Airbnb platform update — major or minor — manually verify that metadata fields haven't been wiped
If using everything sync: confirm all listing details are pushing correctly from your PMS
If using pricing & availability sync only: text, amenity tags, and category markers are managed directly on Airbnb
API sync health check is scheduled as a recurring calendar task — not something you only check when things break
